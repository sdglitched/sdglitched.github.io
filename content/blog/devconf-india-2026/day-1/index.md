## Day 1 Highlights (TLDR):
- Booth setup complete by 10:30 AM
- Met [Matthew Miller](https://mattdm.org/) ([Ex-FPL](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/council/fpl/))
- Answered countless questions about Fedora from budding Linux enthusiasts

- Squeezed in a quick 15-minute lunch (after climbing 8 floors!)

## The Morning Rush

The day started with a couple of unexpected situations. First, [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) mentioned that he wouldn’t be able to drop by my place to do an inventory check of the swag packs that were delivered there. This meant I also had to make an unusual purchase of double-sided tape to ensure we could paste fliers at the [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/) booth. Not the most glamorous start, but hey, conference prep is all about the details!

After waiting for a while, [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) finally arrived and we were on our way to the venue [MIT World Peace University (MIT WPU)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/F1Ju9MJaSqCNMpsV8), arriving around **10:00 AM**. But to our surprise, the venue had been moved to the newly constructed Vyas Building, which meant we had to walk over a kilometer carrying the swag packs. To our relief, [Avadhoot Dhere](https://www.linkedin.com/in/avadhoot-dhere-a035801a2/) was there at the building’s entrance and helped us carry our load to the [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/) booth.

By **10:30 AM**, our booth was up and running.

## Talking to the Budding Linux Crowd

This is where things got real. As someone who primarily contributes to different Fedora applications mostly on backend systems and infrastructure code, I wasn't used to being the person **explaining Fedora to curious attendees**. But there I was, answering questions about:

- What is Fedora and how is it different from other distros?
- What are Fedora's core principles (Freedom, Friends, Features, First)?
- Why should someone choose Fedora over Ubuntu, Arch, or Debian?
- How can I contribute to Fedora?

The questions came fast and furious from enthusiastic students, developers and open-source newcomers. Some were **budding Linux users** who had just started their journey, while others were seasoned contributors looking to get involved with Fedora specifically.

## Swag, Trivia and Digital Badges

Throughout the day, we:

- Informed attendees about **digital badges** they could claim by signing up
- Ran a **trivia** with questions about [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/) basics
- Distributed swag including:
  - Physical badge cum fridge magnets
  - Stickers
  - Digital badges in their [FAS (Fedora Account System)](https://accounts.fedoraproject.org/) accounts

The **special swag** (a Fedora-branded sipper) was reserved for those who scored perfect score in the trivia **and** had good luck with the spin wheel!

## Lunch Break (If You Can Call It That)

While all this was happening, [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) and I left [Samyak](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Jnsamyak) and [Yashwanth](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Thisisyaash) to handle the booth. During this time, the footfall was reduced as most of the attendees had left for lunch.

After climbing **8 floors** through sixteen flights of stairs along with [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der), we reached the dining establishment arranged by [DevConf.IN](https://www.devconf.info/in/), which volunteer Rahul Sharma had informed us about around **01:00 PM**. We did end up wasting our time though, since we found a huge queue at that place.

While coming back disappointed and hungry we met Pravin Satpute, who suggested that we have lunch at the cafeteria on the fifth floor. Due to this small suggestion, [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) and I finally managed to squeeze in a **15 minutes** lunch break along with [Avadhoot Dhere](https://www.linkedin.com/in/avadhoot-dhere-a035801a2/). Yes, you read that right **15 minutes!!** Conference life is no joke!

The dining choice was limited to a *Vegetarian Biryani* and *Cold Coffee*, but at around **02:30 PM**, that felt like a divine serving.

After coming back, we switched places with [Samyak](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Jnsamyak) and [Yashwanth](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Thisisyaash) so they could have lunch as well.

## Booth Hopping

## Wrapping Up Day 1

By the end of Day 1, I was exhausted but energized. The experience of talking to so many people, explaining open-source philosophy and representing [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/) was surreal.

All 4 of us walked together to the gate and started booking cabs/autos to get back to our homes/hotels to freshen up and take a bit of rest before we headed out for the community dinner.

## Community Dinner at Wasabi 15

{{< figure src="images/community_dinner.jpeg" alt="Community Dinner at Wasabi 15" caption="Community Dinner at @Wasabi 15 (Akashdeep Dhar, CC BY-SA 4.0)" align="center" >}}

Community dinner was planned at [Wasabi 15](https://maps.app.goo.gl/bsG43AMg8ELh2JKk9), Viman Nagar, near Pune airport. After freshening up, getting some rest and reflecting on the surreal experience, it was time to get ready again for the much-awaited dinner after our **15 minute** lunch.

Finding a cab/auto proved to be quite challenging with the ongoing issues with cab/auto drivers in Pune these days. Due to the delays, we all agreed to postpone the meeting time from 8:00 PM to 8:30 PM. [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) made the changes to the reservation and confirmed with us.

I finally got a cab and arrived around 8:25 PM. [Samyak](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Jnsamyak) and [Yashwanth](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Thisisyaash) were already there. As I was heading up, [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) also arrived. We went up together and met [Samyak](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Jnsamyak) and [Yashwanth](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Thisisyaash).

Since we were already running a bit late and it had been a long day, we quickly started checking the menu and ordering food. For starters, [Samyak](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Jnsamyak) chose Asparagus Cream Cheese Sushi and Peri Peri French Fries to suit his dietary preferences, while the rest of us ordered Dakgangjeong, Korean Fried Chicken, Tuna Mayo Gimbap and Chicken Kimchi Gimbap.

While waiting for our food to arrive, [Yashwanth](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Thisisyaash) shared his early open source program experiences. All three of us could sense his frustration with the use of AI while contributing to open source programs like Outreachy and GSoC.

I shared my experience contributing to the [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/) and how the maintainers approach AI contributions. [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der) explained that in the [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/), they don't just evaluate PR quality - they also observe contributor behavior. They notice everything, including whether contributors ask for help or help others.

Meanwhile, our food arrived and we started digging in. It was my first time trying [Wasabi 15](https://maps.app.goo.gl/bsG43AMg8ELh2JKk9) and to their credit, they did not disappoint. Afterwards, we talked more about the day's events and what we could have done better.

For the main course, we ordered Stir Fry Ramen in Gangjang Sauce with Batter Fried Tofu for [Samyak](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Jnsamyak), Stir Fry Ramen in Teriyaki Sauce with Batter Fried Prawns for [Akash](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:T0xic0der), Chicken Kung Pao with Butter Garlic Rice for [Yashwanth](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/User:Thisisyaash) and Stir Fry Ramen in Gangjang Sauce with Batter Fried Chicken for me. Once again, [Wasabi 15](https://maps.app.goo.gl/bsG43AMg8ELh2JKk9) delivered.
